Categories of abuse-deterrent formulation clinical studies

The 2013 FDA Draft Guidance for Industry identified specific criteria by which the abuse-deterrent characteristics of an opioid formulation can be evaluated. These studies should:

  • Be scientifically rigorous
  • Consider the most common routes of abuse of the opioid1

FDA Categories for Characterizing Abuse Deterrence1

*“Overall drug liking” measures the user’s retrospective assessment of a drug, whereas “VAS for drug liking” measures the user’s immediate assessment.

Labeling of opioid formulations with abuse-deterrent properties1

FDA has developed a 4-tier system for labeling regarding claims describing the potential abuse-deterrent properties of an opioid formulation. Formulations are classified from Tier 1 to Tier 4, with Tier 4 having the greatest clinical support.

Four Tiers of Labeling Claims1

These 4 tiers correspond to the 4 categories of clinical studies for abuse-deterrent formulations previously outlined. However, it is important to note that FDA generally expects pharmaceutical companies to provide data from clinical studies in Categories 1, 2, and 3 to be eligible for Tier 1, Tier 2, or Tier 3 labeling claims. That is, data from Category 1 clinical studies alone may not be enough to support a Tier 1 labeling claim; data from Category 2 or Category 3 (or both) may also be needed to ensure that a Tier 1 labeling claim is not misleading.1

Prescribing opioids with abuse-deterrent properties

When considering prescribing an opioid with potential abuse-deterrent properties for your patients, refer to the product’s Full Prescribing Information; relevant information may be found in Section 9.2. The labeling may contain clinical, in vitro, and pharmacokinetics study data that support the product’s potential abuse-deterrent properties.1 For more information on these study descriptions, see FDA Categories for Characterizing Abuse Deterrence.
